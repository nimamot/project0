intrestinng facts about provinces:

  1. British Columbia is the third largest and most westerly province in Canada and it is four times the size of Great Britain. The length of British Colombia's coastline is over 27,000 kilometres.
  2. Half of all British Columbians live in the metropolitan area of Vancouver.
  3. Two-thirds of British Columbia's land base (we are speaking of an area larger than France!) is forest land, and more than half of that area has had little or no human contact.
  4. over half of the population of Alberta lives in only two cities.
    • 1,239,220 in Calgary
    • 932,546 in Edmonton
  5. Alberta has the oldest national park in Canada.
  6. There are over 100,000 lakes in Manatoba.
  7. Ontario is the nickel capital of the world.
  8. the official language in Quebec is French instead of English.
  9. Nunavut did not exist until 1999.
  10. Nova Scotia used to be rule by French.

Alberta

Adopted on June 1, 1968, the flag of Alberta contains the Shield centred on an azure-blue background. The shield is topped by St. George's Cross on a white background, representing King George III and symbolizing British heritage.

British Columbia

The four wavy white and three wavy blue lines symbolize the province's location between the Pacific Ocean and the Rocky Mountains. The setting sun represents the fact that British Columbia is Canada's westernmost province
